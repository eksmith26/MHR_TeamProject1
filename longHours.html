Analysis and Synthesis of Tesla Stressors

Long Hours and Overtime are Expected

Multiple ex-employees and reporters have commented on Tesla’s unique factory work hours. Blogs and News articles have analyzed the stress that the long hours and high expectations put on the workers.

Ex factory workers explained Tesla’s working schedule as follows; Tesla operates on an alternating schedule of 3-4-4-3 working days, with a minimum of 10 hours per day and often pushing up to 12 hours per day (sometimes mandatory). It’s not uncommon for employees to work 5 or 6 days a week, including weekends and holidays.

Tesla’s work schedule is unusual not just among businesses but among the automotive industry. 10 hours for four days is standard in the automotive industry, 12 hours is not, as employees will lose efficiency because such long times wear people out.

Some of the effects of a supra - 40 hour workweek on the body include, Heart disease, hypertension, anxiety, lack of sleep and bad habits.

The expectation at Tesla is that you work over 40 hours a week, which creates a lot of pressure on having to work a lot every day. It’s difficult enough to get a job at Tesla. The stress level at Tesla will depend on the team or a manager and how much they allow their team to burn out during crunch times. Thus the stress level at Tesla is heavily dependent on the specific role and department that you’re in.
